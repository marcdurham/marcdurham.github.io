--- layout: post title: Negative 15 Degrees and Smoky date: '2016-01-16T17:57:00.001-08:00' author: Marc Durham tags: modified_time: '2016-01-16T17:57:37.899-08:00' thumbnail: http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-6M3L8Ta5d9I/Vprx5yXKT_I/AAAAAAABkpQ/Q2TreJWCk10/s72-c/IMG_20160117_094123.jpg blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-6813567.post-7194521889281899651 --- Sometimes I can't believe I live in a place where the weather tile on my phone says "-15 Degrees, Smoke."  But I do.

We complain about it a lot, or we brag about it: "Wow the AQI is 400 today!"  And everybody is impressed because it's one of those "AQI 400" days.  (By the way the AQI in Bellingham was 23 the last time we checked, above 200 is considered "hazardous." AQI stands for Air Quality Index or something like that.)

But all we're really sure it means is that we'll have something to blame if we cough, or if we get sick.  Really we're never sure exactly what the bad AQI is doing to us, we just know that it smells smoky, and that it's not good for us.

After about three weeks we had to tape the gaps in our triple pane windows with duct tape.  The AQI in our bedroom was about 450.  We know this because a friend of ours had an AQI meter and let us measure our AQI.  He also lent us his second air purifier.  I was sick for a few days with a fever
